Short Bio

I received a B.Eng. degree in Computer Engineering, in 2016, and an M.Sc. degree in Computer Science, in 2018, both from Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro (Brazil). In 2021, I received a Ph.D. degree in Computer Science from Université Côte d'Azur (France), having EURECOM and Inria - Sophia Antipolis as hosting research institutions. As of January 2022, I am with the MORE research department at Orange Innovation as a postdoctoral researcher, where I work on resource allocation problems for future networks. In November 2022, I joined Université Paris Dauphine as a lecturer in charge of the Computer Networking course for both undergraduate and master levels.

My research interests are in the modeling and optimization of networked systems. I have investigated problems in Edge Computing and Networking, ranging from Edge-Caching, to resource allocation for latency-sensitive applications. My approaches include static optimization (classic and stochastic) and dynamic policies.
